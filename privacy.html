SnapSplit Privacy Policy Last updated: 2026-05-01 SnapSplit is an iOS app that helps you scan menus and receipts with AI and split bills with friends. This page explains what data the app uses, where it goes, and how to remove it. 1. Data we collect from your device Photos you scan or import • When you tap the scan button, the app captures or imports a photo. The photo is sent over HTTPS to our backend service hosted on Google Cloud Run (menu-parse-api-848728236997.asia-east1.run.app) and processed by Google Cloud Vertex AI (Gemini) to detect items and prices. • Compressed photo bytes are uploaded for parsing. Photos are not used for advertising or for training third-party models beyond what Google's Vertex AI terms describe. • Parsed results, image thumbnails, and originals (if you opt in) are stored locally on your device using SwiftData. You can wipe them at any time from Settings → Clear all local data. Location data • If you grant Location When In Use, the app may use your current GPS coordinates to find nearby stores and suggest a match for the scanned menu or receipt. • If a photo from your library contains EXIF GPS metadata, those coordinates may be sent alongside the photo to help match the correct store. • Location data is used only for store matching. It is not used for advertising, profiling, or shared with third parties. Microphone & Speech Recognition • If you tap the voice search button, the app records audio via the system microphone and uses Apple's on-device or Apple-hosted Speech Recognition to convert it to text. The transcribed text is used only to fill the search field. Audio is not stored or uploaded by SnapSplit. Diagnostic logs • The app keeps a local log of API calls and errors. Logs are uploaded to our backend (POST /v1/client-logs) only when you explicitly trigger an upload from the in-app developer tools. Logs include a per-device vendor identifier (UIDevice.identifierForVendor) that resets when you uninstall the app. Logs do not include your name, account, or photos. 2. Data we do not collect • No account, email, password, or sign-in is required. • No advertising identifiers (IDFA) are read. • No third-party analytics or crash SDKs (no Firebase, Sentry, Crashlytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, etc.). • No payment or banking information is collected by SnapSplit. In-app purchases for SnapSplit Pro are processed entirely by Apple's StoreKit; we never see your card details. 3. Where data is processed • On your device: Local storage of menus, receipts, sessions, store cache, and preferences via SwiftData / UserDefaults. • Our backend (Google Cloud Run, Asia-East1 region): Receives the uploaded photo and metadata, calls Vertex AI, returns parsed JSON. • Google Cloud Vertex AI (Gemini): Performs OCR / parsing. Subject to Google's data handling and retention terms. 4. Data retention & deletion • Your scanned photos, parsed items, sessions, and store records live on your device. You can soft-delete everything from Settings → Clear all local data; deleted items are recoverable for 7 days, then removed permanently. • Uninstalling SnapSplit removes all locally stored data. • When you submit a photo for parsing, we may retain the following on our backend, associated only with a per-vendor device identifier (identifierForVendor) — never with your name, email, or any account: ◦ Anonymous parse metadata (request id, model, parse result, warnings, timing) — up to 7 days, used to monitor quality and improve our prompts. ◦ A small thumbnail of the uploaded image (≤ 256 px, keyed by SHA-256 hash) — up to 90 days, used to investigate parse failures and de-duplicate identical images. ◦ The SHA-256 hash of the uploaded image — retained indefinitely so that a previously seen image can be matched without re-uploading or re-processing the original. • Photo originals are not retained by default. A future version of the app may offer an opt-in setting to retain originals to help improve recognition; until you explicitly enable such a setting, originals are discarded after parsing. 5. Children SnapSplit is not directed at children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with data, please contact us and we will delete it. 6. Your rights You can clear all on-device data from Settings at any time. Because we do not maintain user accounts, there is no server-side profile to delete. 7. Changes to this policy If this policy materially changes, the updated version will appear at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. 8. Contact Questions? Email yl.appstudio@icloud.com .
